 
                           What We're Building                     Best in B2B          is an invitation-only, on-location video podcast for senior B2B leaders across Dallas–Fort Worth. It is the flagship content product of EXL Ventures LLC.                                                The Show                   A professionally produced video series featuring CMOs, VPs of Marketing, and CEOs at mid-market B2B tech companies in DFW. Every episode is a single 45-minute on-location conversation — filmed at the guest's office using a dual-camera Sony FX3A setup with broadcast-quality audio. No studio. No remote recordings. No generic talking heads.                   The name is            Best in B2B         . Domain is            b2bbest.show         . Social handle is            @b2bbest         . Season 1 is limited to 12 founding guests.                                                The Business Model                   The show is a Trojan Horse. On the surface it's editorial content. Underneath it's EXL's most powerful sales channel.                   Every CMO or VP who sits in that chair becomes a warm relationship. Not a cold lead — a person who spent 45 minutes talking strategy with the EXL team, received $10,000–$15,000 worth of production value for free, and now has a personal reason to think of EXL when budget opens up. The conversion target is a production retainer at $2,500–$7,500 per month.                   The show also has its own revenue ceiling long-term — sponsorships, a paid newsletter on Beehiiv, the Best in B2B Awards, a live DFW summit, a paid community on Circle.so, and research reports. But none of that is the priority right now. Right now the show exists to build the pipeline.                                                The Positioning                   Invitation only. Curated. Editorial. The copy and the page treat it like a            D CEO          feature or a            Forbes          profile series — not a podcast looking for guests. Guests don't apply. They request consideration. We decide.                   This positioning does three things simultaneously. It flatters the executive into wanting to be part of it. It filters for quality so every episode is actually good. And it makes EXL look like a           premium production house, not a scrappy agency trying to get camera time with decision-makers.                   The core competitive advantage — the one thing zero DFW competitors are doing — is            on-location production         . Everyone else records remotely or in a generic studio. We show up at their office with a full crew. That's the differentiator that justifies the invitation-only framing and makes the content actually worth watching.                                                The Brand                     Best in B2B          runs visually separate from EXL but shares the same color DNA — chartreuse            #D4FF00          on dark. The logo is a wordmark-first design currently being developed by designer Hossam. Three concept directions are in brief: EXL Family, AI Era, and Fresh Identity. The brand identity deliverables include social covers, YouTube thumbnail template, guest share kit, episode clip template, email signature, and a branded PowerPoint.
